 Chess is a board game between two players. It is sometimes called international chess or Western chess to distinguish it from related games, such as xiangqi Chinese chess and shogi Japanese chess. Chess originated as chaturanga in ancient India and from there it made its way to Persia, the Byzantine empire and the Arab world. Muslims brought chess to Europe via Spain (Al Andalus) and Italy (Emirate of Sicily). Eastern Slavs spread it to the Kievan Rus and the Vikings carried the game as far as Iceland and England. The rules of the game evolved over the centuries, with the last major changes being introduced in the 15th century and winning acceptance by the 19th century.[1]
